About Me

I'm a Master of Science in Information Systems (MSIS) student at the Kelley School of Business, where I blend research, analytics, and systems thinking to solve complex problems and create meaningful connections. I'm driven by curiosity and collaboration, with a belief that technology should be as human as it is innovative.

My professional interests center on using technology to enable smarter, more adaptive organizations. Whether through systems analysis, technology consulting, or data-driven strategy, I love bridging the gap between people and tech to make ideas tangible and impactful.

Outside of academics and work, I'm big on balance — I love to lift, run, and spend time outdoors to clear my head. I'm also a fan of horror and indie films (the weirder, the better), and I never miss a chance to travel. Cusco has been my favorite destination so far, and I'm convinced New Orleans has the best food on the planet.

I'm always looking for new challenges and forward-thinking environments where I can help build solutions that make a real difference — and enjoy the journey along the way.
